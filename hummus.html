Hummus Reviews

I like hummus. Here are my (and friends) reviews of store-bought hummus brands around Vancouver. Based off of Arielle's apple ratings.

Note: All hummuses are tasted with Real Foods Corn Thins (Flax & Soy).

  • fontaine sante, traditional
    • 7.5 (by definition, acts as a baseline for comparison)
    • morgan: very bright flavor, love the lemon. this brand/flavor is the reason I love hummus
    • shen: i cannot lie. do somehow forget what it tastes like every time until i put in it in my mouth and then i fully understand how morgan ate the entire tub i bought (that he said he did not want any of) AND half of my subsequent hummus reparations. the flavour hits u with the perfect amount of lemon.. consistency never misses.. a classic for a reason n truly fun for the whole family.. they need to sell this in 2kg tubs again..
  • fontaine sante, roasted red pepper
    • morgan (6): the whole time im just wishing it was the traditional flavour, tastes like too vegetable-y
    • shen (5.5): i am not one to go hate on vegetables. some may even call me a vegetable appreciator. but this tastes like when you bite into an orange bell pepper like it's an apple and chew it for a little too long. and then if you spooned some hummus into your mouth as an afterthought. like a mom sneaking broccoli into her picky kid's mac n cheese. not the worst thing in the world but if i am eating veggies, i would like to eat them knowing that it was a decision i made on my own. am also probably eating this with veggies anyways. so. do not appreciate this (rather unnecessary) flavour addition to an otherwise solid hummus. also do not appreciate people who say that peppers are technically fruit. one more thing. where is the roasted aspect to this. all that being said. would not be mad if i had to eat this. but would certainly not be my first choice!!
  • habibi's basil & garlic
    • morgan (5): woah, lots of basil and garlic. hard to open the package, and much thicker than the fontaine sante varieties (almost broke the cracker). hummus should be about chickpeas & lemon, this is not that (feels like its trying to distract you from those)
    • shen (5): can this really be classified as hummus.. Much To Consider.. consistency reminds me of what i think about when i think about grout. dipping things into this requires maximum precision due to the sheer gravitas (and gravitational pull) that it possesses.. as does breaking the plastic seal when you open this bad boy up.. with that being said!! tastes great. you cannot go wrong with garlic or basil. and it might be some sunk cost fallacy stockholm syndrome logic kicking in but this flavour bomb paste holds may be well worth the effort you put into consuming it.. a good post-arm day cooldown, if you will!!
